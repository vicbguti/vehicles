# Problem 2: Geographic Demand Disparity & Supply Chain Misalignment

## The Real-World Problem
Car manufacturers, dealerships, and local governments face high costs and inefficiencies because they cannot predict regional demand changes:
* **For Dealerships/Distributors**: Allocating the wrong mix of vehicle types (e.g., sending high-end SUVs to rural cantons or small sedans to mountainous terrains) results in high inventory holding costs, depreciation, and missed sales opportunities.
* **For Local Governments**: Municipalities (cantons) rely on vehicle tax revenues for budgeting. Sudden shifts in vehicle sales lead to unexpected fiscal deficits or infrastructure funding misallocations.
